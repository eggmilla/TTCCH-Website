ABOUT US

Why we exist

Australia has long had foster homes for people, cats, dogs and wildlife. We thought “Why not farmed animals too?”. Today ‘Til The Cows Come Home is Australia’s leading online adoption agency for farmed animals. We have foster carers across Australia enabling adopters to turn their homes into forever homes for these deserving souls. From ducks to camels, feather, fur or skin we help them all.

Our goal

Our mission is to save unwanted farmed animals by working directly with farmers and families to rehome them into loving, forever homes. Our sophisticated adoption process and foster care program together make for a world where people are progressively viewing farmed animals with the same warmth, and to offer the same rights, afforded to domestic pets.

Every life that TTCCH touches, we change their world forever.

Who we are

Cathy Blasonato

Cathy has been involved in animal welfare for many years, both as a volunteer and on a professional basis. She spent five years as Supporter Services Manager and Grants Manager for the International Fund for Animal Welfare (AUS.) As well as being a board member for TTCCH she is also on the board of Soi Dog Australia. Cathy is a delegate for the Animal Justice Party (AJP) NSW and a regional group leader. She also stood for the AJP in the 2019 state election in the seat of Ballina.

Melissa Hobbs

Melissa is a committed animal rights campaigner, and the co-founder of The Vegan Company and Meat Free Week. Some of Melissa’s work in the field of animal welfare includes the strategy for the relaunch of the RSPCA’s Choose Wisely campaign. She has also enjoyed a successful career in magazine publishing, holding senior positions in advertising and strategy at News Limited.

Sarah Tivendale

Sarah has a passion for animal welfare and has previously worked as a volunteer for ‘Til The Cows Come Home. She has a background in the banking and financial sector that includes work with Morgan’s Financial and the ME Bank as an administration and settlement officer. Her roles with ‘Til The Cows Come Home include managing adoptions and enquiries and working with foster carers to ensure the best possible outcome for re-homed animals.

Phoebe Wisken

Phoebe has been an animal carer since she was 12 years old. She has previously worked as a volunteer for Animal Welfare League, W.I.R.E.S and Riding for the Disabled. Phoebe is currently training to be a nurse and a midwife. Her knowledge of health care makes her an invaluable part of the team.
