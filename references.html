I took inspiration from government sites such as my aged care and Victoria's ombudsman as they are rated as highly accessible. Drawing upon their similarities, I discoverd that they:

  • use colour blocking
  • have lots of white space
  • use only one typeface with different weights and sizes
  • have links or buttons to reveal more information
I wanted to take these into account when creating my own website in order to be more inclusive. The initial changes I made were:
  • using two typefaces
  • not using a cow print pattern background as it could be very distracting even if static
  • creating obvious hover states for all links - used underline and colour change for text
  • utilising white space

From retail sites I saw the use of animation and call-to-actions and was inspired to possibly include some of these on my site. I included a 'donate now' button on the landing page as the header doesn't feature an explicit 'donate' page.

  • having a slightly see-through hero textbox
